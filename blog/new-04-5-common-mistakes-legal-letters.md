# 5 Common Mistakes People Make When Sending Legal Letters

**Meta Title:** 5 Common Mistakes When Sending Legal Letters (And How to Avoid Them)  
**Meta Description:** Don’t weaken your position. Learn the top mistakes people make with demand letters and cease and desist letters — and how to send them correctly.

## Introduction

Sending a legal letter is a powerful step, but many people make mistakes that reduce its effectiveness or even hurt their case. Here are the five most common errors and how to avoid them.

## 1. Being Too Emotional or Aggressive

Threatening language, insults, or overly dramatic wording can make you look unreasonable. It may also give the other side grounds to claim harassment.

**Better approach:** Stay calm, factual, and professional.

## 2. Being Vague About What You Want

Many letters fail to clearly state exactly what action is required and by when.

**Better approach:** Be specific. Example: “Pay the outstanding balance of $3,450 by June 15, 2026.”

## 3. Not Including Supporting Evidence

Just making claims without attaching or referencing contracts, invoices, messages, or photos weakens your letter.

**Better approach:** Reference and attach key documents.

## 4. Setting Unrealistic or No Deadlines

No deadline or an unreasonably short one reduces the letter’s impact.

**Better approach:** Give a reasonable timeframe (usually 7–21 days depending on the situation).

## 5. Sending Without Professional Review

DIY letters sometimes contain errors, weak language, or statements that can be used against you later.

**Better approach:** Have the letter reviewed (or drafted) by an attorney before sending.

## Bonus Tip

Always keep copies and send via a method that gives you proof of delivery (certified mail or tracked email).
