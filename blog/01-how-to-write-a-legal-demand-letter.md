## What Is a Legal Demand Letter?

A legal demand letter is a formal written notice sent to another person or business demanding that they take (or stop taking) a specific action. It usually includes:

- A clear statement of the problem
- The legal basis for your claim
- What you want them to do (pay money, return property, stop certain behavior, etc.)
- A deadline
- Consequences if they fail to comply

Demand letters are commonly used in:
- Unpaid invoices or loans
- Breach of contract
- Property damage
- Personal injury claims
- Landlord-tenant disputes
- Intellectual property issues

## When Should You Send a Demand Letter?

You should consider sending a demand letter when:
- Informal requests have been ignored
- You want to create a formal record of your claim
- You want to give the other party a chance to resolve the issue before court
- You need to meet certain legal prerequisites before filing a lawsuit

**Important:** Sending a demand letter does **not** start a lawsuit, but it can be powerful evidence if the matter later goes to court.

## Step-by-Step: How to Write an Effective Legal Demand Letter

### 1. Use Professional Letter Format
Include:
- Your full contact information
- Date
- Recipient’s full name and address
- Clear subject line (e.g., “Formal Demand for Payment of Outstanding Invoice #1234”)

### 2. Clearly State the Facts
Be factual and chronological. Avoid emotional language. Stick to:
- What happened
- When it happened
- Who was involved
- Supporting evidence (contracts, invoices, photos, messages, etc.)

### 3. Explain the Legal Basis
Briefly state why the other party is legally obligated to act. You don’t need to write like a lawyer, but reference relevant agreements or laws if you know them.

### 4. Make a Specific Demand
Be precise about what you want:
- Exact amount of money
- Specific action (e.g., “repair the damage by June 15”)
- Deadline (usually 7–30 days is reasonable)

### 5. State the Consequences
Politely but firmly explain what you will do if they don’t comply (e.g., “I will have no choice but to pursue legal action”).

### 6. Include Supporting Documents
Mention and attach copies of relevant documents (never send originals).

### 7. Close Professionally
End with a clear call to action and your contact information.

## Sample Structure of a Demand Letter

```
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[City, State, ZIP]
[Email] | [Phone]
[Date]

[Recipient Name]
[Recipient Address]

Re: Demand for Payment of $4,850 – Invoice #INV-2847 dated March 12, 2026

Dear [Name],

I am writing to formally demand payment of the outstanding balance of $4,850 for services rendered...

[Body with facts, legal basis, demand, deadline, and consequences]

Please remit payment by [Date]. If I do not receive payment or a satisfactory response by this date, I will pursue all available legal remedies...

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Being overly aggressive or threatening
- Including false statements
- Being vague about what you want
- Missing deadlines or evidence
- Sending without keeping copies and proof of delivery (use certified mail or email with read receipt)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long should I wait for a response to a demand letter?**  
Most demand letters give 7 to 30 days. 14–21 days is common for payment demands.

**Is a demand letter legally binding?**  
No, but it creates an official record and can be used as evidence in court.

**Can I send a demand letter by email?**  
Yes, but certified mail with return receipt or email with delivery/read confirmation is stronger for proof.

**Do I need a lawyer to send a demand letter?**  
Not required, but having one review or draft it significantly increases its effectiveness.
