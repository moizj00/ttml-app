# Cease and Desist Letters Explained: What They Are and When to Use Them

## Introduction

A **cease and desist letter** is one of the most common legal tools used to stop unwanted behavior. Whether someone is harassing you, infringing on your intellectual property, spreading false information, or interfering with your business, a cease and desist letter puts them on formal notice.

At Talk to My Lawyer, we help people send professional, attorney-reviewed cease and desist letters.

## What Is a Cease and Desist Letter?

A cease and desist letter is a formal written demand that tells another person or business to:

- **Stop** (cease) doing something harmful or illegal
- **Not do it again** (desist)

It usually explains:
- What the offending behavior is
- Why it’s problematic (legal or factual reasons)
- What you want them to stop doing
- A deadline
- Potential consequences if they continue

## Common Situations Where Cease and Desist Letters Are Used

- **Harassment or threats**
- **Defamation** (false statements that harm your reputation)
- **Intellectual property infringement** (copyright, trademark, or trade secret violations)
- **Unfair competition** or business interference
- **Trespassing**
- **Breach of non-compete or confidentiality agreements**
- **Repeated unwanted contact**

## What a Cease and Desist Letter Can and Cannot Do

**It can:**
- Formally notify the person of the problem
- Create a paper trail
- Often stop the behavior (many people comply when they receive a professional letter)
- Strengthen your position if you later need to go to court

**It cannot:**
- Force someone to stop immediately by itself (it’s not a court order)
- Guarantee the behavior will stop
- Replace the need for a lawsuit in serious or ongoing cases

## Why Attorney Review Is Important

A poorly worded cease and desist letter can sometimes make the situation worse or expose you to claims (for example, if it contains threats or false statements). An attorney-reviewed letter is clearer, more professional, and legally sound.
