# What Is a Demand Letter and When Should You Send One?

**Meta Title:** What Is a Demand Letter? When to Send One (And Why It Works)  
**Meta Description:** A clear explanation of what a demand letter is, when you should send one, and how an attorney-reviewed demand letter can help resolve disputes without court.

## Introduction

If someone owes you money, breaks an agreement, or violates your rights, one of the most effective first steps you can take is sending a **demand letter**.

A demand letter is a formal written notice that clearly states what you want and gives the other party a chance to resolve the issue before you escalate to legal action.

## What Exactly Is a Demand Letter?

A demand letter is a written document that:

- Clearly explains the problem or dispute
- States the legal or factual basis for your claim
- Specifies exactly what you want the other party to do (pay money, stop certain behavior, fulfill an obligation, etc.)
- Sets a reasonable deadline
- Explains what you will do if they don’t comply

It serves as both a **formal request** and a **paper trail** that can be very useful if the matter later goes to court.

## When Should You Send a Demand Letter?

You should consider sending a demand letter when:

- Informal requests (texts, emails, calls) have been ignored
- You want to create an official record of your claim
- You want to give the other person or business one last chance to resolve things amicably
- You’re dealing with unpaid invoices, contract breaches, property damage, or similar issues
- You want to strengthen your position before considering a lawsuit

**Common situations** where demand letters are used:
- Unpaid freelance or contractor invoices
- Security deposit disputes with landlords
- Breach of contract
- Property damage claims
- Debt collection (you sending, not receiving)
- Business disputes

## Why Demand Letters Are Often Effective

Many disputes get resolved after a well-written demand letter because:

- It shows you’re serious
- It creates a clear record
- It gives the other party a chance to fix the problem without going to court
- Courts often look favorably on people who tried to resolve things reasonably first

## Should You Write It Yourself or Get It Attorney-Reviewed?

You *can* write a basic demand letter yourself. However, having it **attorney-reviewed** (or drafted) significantly increases its effectiveness and protects you from saying something that could hurt your case later.

Professional letters tend to get faster and better responses.
