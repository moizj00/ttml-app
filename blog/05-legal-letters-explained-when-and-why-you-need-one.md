# Legal Letters Explained: When and Why You Might Need One

**Meta Title:** Legal Letters Explained: Types, When to Use Them, and Why Attorney Review Matters  
**Meta Description:** A clear guide to common legal letters — demand letters, cease and desist, and more. Learn when to send them and why professional review is often worth it.

## Introduction

Legal letters are one of the most effective and underused tools for resolving disputes without going to court. Whether you need to demand payment, stop harmful behavior, or formally assert your rights, the right letter sent at the right time can resolve issues quickly.

## Common Types of Legal Letters

- **Demand Letters** — Most common. Used to demand money, action, or compliance.
- **Cease and Desist Letters** — Used to stop someone from doing something (defamation, IP infringement, harassment, etc.).
- **Breach of Contract Letters** — Formal notice of contract violation.
- **Response Letters** — Replying to a letter you received.

## Why Attorney Review Matters

A well-drafted, attorney-reviewed letter carries more weight and reduces the risk of saying something that could hurt your position later.

## When You Should Consider Sending One

- Unpaid invoices or money owed
- Someone is violating your rights
- Contract disputes
- Protecting your business or reputation
