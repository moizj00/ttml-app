## 4. Social Media Content Pillars

**Posting frequency:** 3x per week

## 5. Newsletter Strategy

### Cadence
- **Frequency:** Weekly (every Tuesday at 10am EST)
- **List segments:** Leads (non-subscribers), Active Subscribers, Past Subscribers (churned)

Each issue:
1. **Lead story:** One practical legal tip or explainer (300–400 words)

## 6. Content Repurposing Waterfall

### Primary Asset: Long-Form Blog Post (~1,500 words)
↓
**Email Newsletter:** Extract the key insight as the lead story (300 words + link to full post)
↓
**LinkedIn Post:** Turn the main finding into a "here's what I learned" narrative (400–600 words)
↓
**Twitter/X Thread:** Break the blog post into 5–8 tweets (numbered thread format)
↓
**Instagram Infographic:** Visualize the key steps or statistics from the post (Canva or Figma)
↓
**Instagram Stories:** 3–5 slides pulling out the most shareable facts
↓
**FAQ Addition:** If the blog answers a common question, add a condensed version to the FAQ page
↓
**Internal Links:** Add a link from the blog post to the most relevant service page (e.g., demand letter → home page)

## 7. Competitor Content Gaps

### Identified Competitor Patterns
Based on the competitive landscape for legal letter services and attorney-reviewed correspondence:

**Competitors typically cover:**
- Generic "how to write a demand letter" guides (high volume, low depth)
- Basic FAQ pages with surface-level answers
- Testimonial pages (often thin)

**Identified gaps Talk to My Lawyer can fill:**

1. **Jurisdiction-specific content:** "Sending a Demand Letter in California vs. Texas: What Changes?" — few competitors break this down by state.

2. **Document analysis explainers:** "How to Read a Legal Letter You've Received" — most services focus on sending letters, not analyzing incoming ones. Our Document Analyzer is a natural fit.

3. **Outcome-focused content:** "What Happens After You Send a Demand Letter — 5 Real Scenarios" — competitors rarely follow the story past the send date.

4. **AI + legal transparency:** "How AI Helps Draft Attorney-Reviewed Letters" — demystifying the AI-human hybrid approach builds trust with skeptical users.

5. **Pricing transparency:** "Why Demand Letters Cost What They Cost" — most services hide pricing or have complex fee structures. Being transparent is a differentiator.

6. **Employment-specific content:** Freelancer disputes, wage theft, non-compete violations — underserved by most competitors who focus on consumer debt/landlord issues.

7. **Counter-letter guidance:** "You Received a Cease and Desist — Now What?" — capturing users on the receiving end of legal letters is an untapped acquisition channel.

## 8. SEO Technical Notes

- All 5 public pages now have `<Helmet>` meta blocks with unique titles (50–60 chars), descriptions (150–160 chars), OG tags, Twitter Card tags, and canonical URLs.
- `sitemap.xml` is served from `/sitemap.xml` (via `client/public/sitemap.xml`).
- `robots.txt` allows all crawlers on public routes and blocks authenticated-only routes.
- JSON-LD structured data added: Organization + WebApplication + WebSite (Home), Service with Offers (Pricing), FAQPage (FAQ — pre-existing), SoftwareApplication (Document Analyzer).
- OG images use absolute URLs (`https://www.talk-to-my-lawyer.com/logo-main.png`).
- As a SPA, pre-rendering or SSR (out of scope) would further boost crawlability for non-Google bots.
